───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1 — THE LOGIC (read once, internalize forever)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every prompt is just physics + specificity.
AI models are trained on real photographs.
Real photographs = real light + real surfaces + real lenses.
The more your prompt describes actual physical conditions,
the more precise the model's output.

The formula is always:
WHAT + WHERE + HOW LIGHT HITS IT + HOW YOU SEE IT + HOW IT FEELS + WHAT IT ISN'T

Vague prompt → model averages everything it has seen.
Specific prompt → model reconstructs a real physical scenario.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2 — ANGLE LOGIC (choose per dish, never guess)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FLAT DISHES (pizza, salad, flatbread, grain bowl, mezze spread):
→ 70-80° from above, near top-down
→ Reason: maximizes texture, pattern, and color layout
→ Height is irrelevant so flatten the perspective

STANDARD DISHES (pasta, steak, soup, rice, fish fillet):
→ 40-45° from above, slight right or left offset
→ Reason: shows depth and sauce behavior without distorting height
→ This is the Wolt/Deliveroo default — works 80% of the time

TALL DISHES (burger, sandwich stack, tacos, layered cake, skewers):
→ 25-35° from above, sometimes pure side at 10-15°
→ Reason: height is the hero — flatten the angle to let it read
→ Never shoot a tall dish from above or you lose what makes it

RULE: angle serves the dish's best physical feature.
Ask: is this dish's hero its COLOR/TEXTURE (go higher)
or its HEIGHT/LAYERS (go lower)?

SECTION 3 — SURFACE LOCK (never change between dishes)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pick ONE and paste verbatim into every single prompt.
Never paraphrase — rephrase equals drift.

OPTION A — Premium Dark (recommended for most menus):
"Dark matte slate stone surface, slightly textured with natural
variation, no pattern, no shine, no reflections.
Same surface every shot."

OPTION B — Warm Casual:
"Warm light oak wood surface, natural grain visible, matte finish,
no varnish shine, no pattern repeat. Same surface every shot."

OPTION C — Clean Modern:
"Matte concrete gray surface, fine texture, no shine,
no pattern, no visible joints. Same surface every shot."

OPTION D — Classic Editorial:
"Off-white linen textile surface, subtle fabric texture,
no pattern, soft matte finish. Same surface every shot."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4 — VESSEL LOCK (define once, never drift)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Define your plate family once for the entire menu.
Every dish uses the same plate family — only swap
vessel TYPE when the dish genuinely demands it
(soup = bowl, steak = plate, etc.)

PLATE LOCK EXAMPLE:
"White ceramic plate, matte finish, no gloss, 26cm diameter,
medium rim thickness, no pattern, no color variation.
Same plate every shot."

BOWL LOCK EXAMPLE:
"Black matte ceramic bowl, 18cm diameter, medium depth,
no shine, no pattern. Same bowl every shot."

BOARD LOCK EXAMPLE (for rustic/sharing dishes only):
"Dark walnut wooden board, rectangular, natural grain,
matte finish, no varnish. Same board every shot."

RULE: resist the urge to match vessel to dish personality.
Consistency across the menu beats individual dish aesthetics.
One plate family = professional menu. Mixed vessels = chaos.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5 — LIGHTING LOCK (paste verbatim every prompt)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Soft diffused natural window light from upper-left 5500K,
subtle fill from right at 1:3 ratio, no hard shadows,
gentle specular highlight on sauce surface and plate rim only.
No flash look. No studio strobe. No ring light.
Same lighting every shot."

WHEN TO OVERRIDE:
Soups and broths → add: "slight steam wisps catching backlight"
Seared proteins → add: "subtle specular on crust surface from key light"
Cold dishes → add: "no steam, no condensation unless specified"
Desserts → add: "soft front fill to lift sugar texture detail"

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6 — CAMERA LOCK (paste verbatim every prompt)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Sony A7IV 100mm macro f/2.8, dish centered with 20% negative
space on left side, 30% of frame is surface visible.
Fine film grain ISO 400. Same lens every shot."

DEPTH OF FIELD RULE:
Hero element (protein, main ingredient) = tack sharp.
Plate rim foreground = slightly soft.
Surface background = soft falloff.
Never full frame sharp — reads as product catalog not editorial.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 7 — COLOR LOCK (paste verbatim every prompt)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Warm highlights, slightly desaturated shadows, natural food
colors preserved — no oversaturation. Greens natural not
boosted. Reds rich not neon. Browns warm not gray.
Fine film texture ISO 400. Same grade every shot."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 8 — FOOD STATE DESCRIPTORS (add to every prompt)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ALWAYS INCLUDE:
"Fresh not wilted. Colors saturated naturally not enhanced.
Sauce applied moment before shoot — wet and glossy not dried.
Food occupies center 60-70% of frame.
Never fill edge to edge."

ADD PER DISH TYPE:
Hot dishes: "subtle steam wisps rising naturally not theatrical"
Cold dishes: "no condensation, no steam, crisp and fresh"
Sauced dishes: "sauce pooling naturally at base, slight gloss"
Grilled/seared: "visible grill marks or crust texture,
                 caramelization on edges"
Salads: "leaves slightly irregular, dressing coating visible,
         not overdressed or soggy looking"
Soups/broths: "surface slightly broken, ingredients visible
               just below surface, garnish floating naturally"
Desserts: "sugar textures sharp, chocolate glossy,
           cream soft not stiff"

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 9 — GARNISH RULE (critical for realism)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Single hero garnish only. One sprig, one slice, or one drizzle.
Placed at 10 o'clock position, naturally dropped not arranged.
Garnish must be an ingredient that belongs to the dish.
No decorative garnish that is not in the recipe."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 10 — MASTER NEGATIVE (paste into every prompt)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"No props. No cutlery unless specified. No human hands.
No text. No watermarks. No artificial studio lighting.
No plastic sheen on food. No symmetrical garnish placement.
No oversaturated colors. No food touching plate rim.
No empty plate space larger than 40%. No multiple garnishes.
No ingredients that do not belong to this dish."
